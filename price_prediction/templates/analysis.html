Price fluctuation of Commodities on the basis of Seasons:

1.Prices tend to be higher in the winter season (December to February) for most commodities. This is likely due to factors such as decreased production due to colder weather. For example, the price of apples increases by 20% in winter, while the price of carrots nearly doubles.

2.Prices tend to be lower in the monsoon season (June to August) for most commodities. This is likely due to increased production during the rainy season. For example, the price of cucumbers drops by 20% in the monsoon season, while the price of tomatoes drops by 10%.

Price fluctuation of Commodities on the basis of Festivals:

1.Prices tend to increase significantly around major festivals in Nepal. For example, the price of apples nearly doubles around Dashain, the largest and longest festival in Nepal. Similarly, the price of garlic nearly doubles around Lhosar, the Tibetan New Year.

2.Prices also tendto fluctuate around smaller festivals. For example, the price of cucumbers increases slightly around Gai Jatra and Ghode Jatra, two festivals that take place close together.

3.Not all festivals have a noticeable impact on prices. For example, the price of tomatoes remains relatively stable throughout the year.
